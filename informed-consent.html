Informed Consent

Title of the Study: Information Sharing in a Color-Guessing Game

Introduction

You are being invited to participate in a research study. It is essential that you understand why the research is being done and what it will involve before you agree to participate. Please take the time to read the following information carefully. You can find a downloadable version of this consent form at this link.

Researchers

  • Alena Buinskaya (Caltech)
  • Charles D. Sprenger (Caltech)

What is the purpose of the study?

This study aims to understand how people decide whether to share information with or withhold it from a group when the group members’ incentives are aligned.

Who is organizing and funding the research?

This study is organized by the researchers at the Division of Humanities and Social Sciences of the California Institute of Technology (Caltech) and funded by Caltech.

Who has reviewed the study?

Caltech's Institutional Review Board (IRB) has determined that this study is exempt from IRB review as stipulated in the Common Rule, Subpart A of 45 CFR 46 and meets Caltech's ethical standards governing the conduct of research.

Participation in the Study

Why have I been chosen?

You are eligible to participate in this study because you registered on Prolific as a participant, confirmed that you live in the United States, and met the study’s standards for Prolific’s approval rate and the number of previous submissions.

Do I have to participate?

Your participation in this study is entirely voluntary. If you do decide to participate, you will be asked to agree to this consent form. You are still free to withdraw your consent and participation at any time for any reason without penalty. If you choose to withdraw, your data collected to that point will be deleted. You can withdraw by contacting the research team using the contact information provided below.

What will happen to me if I participate?

If you decide to participate, we will ask you to play a color-guessing game. After the game, we will ask you a few questions about the game and your decision-making process.

By its nature, the game contains an element of chance, which may affect the final amount of money you can earn. The element of chance is introduced to simulate real-world decision-making scenarios and is not intended to promote or simulate gambling. However, we recognize that some individuals may have personal, financial, or religious reasons for avoiding activities that resemble gambling. The monetary stakes involved in this game are meant solely for the purpose of this academic research and are not intended for entertainment or gambling purposes.

If you believe that participating in this study conflicts with your personal beliefs or values, please consider carefully whether you wish to proceed with participation. Participation is entirely voluntary, and you are free to withdraw from the study at any time without penalty.

How long will the study take?

Most people will complete this study within 30 minutes.

How much will you pay me?

The compensation you receive for participating in this study can range between $4 and $9, but we expect participants to earn $8 on average.

What are the possible disadvantages and risks of participation?

We do not anticipate any risks or discomforts from participating in this research.

What are the possible benefits of taking part?

There are no direct benefits to you from participating in this study besides the monetary compensation. However, information from this study will help us better understand people’s decision to share information with or withhold it from their group when the group members’ incentives are aligned.

How do you protect the data and ensure its confidentiality?

Your data will be processed in accordance with the California Consumer Privacy Act (CA Civ Code § 1798.100) and Caltech's ethical standards governing the conduct of research. The information you provide will be kept confidential and will not be shared with third parties without your permission. Your data will be anonymized before being analyzed, and any publication of the results will not include information that can identify you. No personal identifiers (including the Participant ID provided by Prolific) will be linked to your data when analyzing or reporting the study results.

How and for how long will the data be stored?

Your data will be stored in encrypted digital format in a password-protected folder accessible only by the research team members and will be kept for duration of the study.

After the study completion, anonymized data may be made available to other researchers online for further research and analysis, under the condition that no personal data can be derived from it.
